Commercial service requests often begin with **missing or incomplete information** where customers provide too little context — leading to wasted visits, repeated clarification, and technician uncertainty.

A customer reports that something is "not working," but critical details are often missing:

* What exactly failed?  
* When did the issue start?  
* Has anything already been tried?  
* Is the issue intermittent?  
* Who is the site contact?  
* Are there access restrictions?  

These missing details create avoidable cost before the technician even arrives.

### Structured Intake Before Dispatch

Instead of relying on a short email or an unstructured phone call, customers are guided through a focused intake conversation that gathers useful context before scheduling.

**This process delivers four key benefits:**

1. **Structured Problem Intake:** Capture symptoms, observations, and customer context in a consistent format.  
2. **Diagnostic Intake Reports:** Receive a clear, job-ready email summary before dispatch.  
3. **Verified Site Details:** Capture business, location, contact, and access information up front.  
4. **Better Visit Preparation:** Give technicians more context before arriving on site.

### Business Advantages

For commercial service teams, structured intake helps:

* **Reduce Unnecessary Visits:** Avoid dispatching without enough information.  
* **Prepare Technicians Earlier:** Improve readiness before rolling a truck.  
* **Reduce Back-and-Forth Communication:** Spend less time clarifying service requests.  
* **Improve First-Visit Success:** Arrive with better context and preparation.  

This is not dispatch software.

This is not scheduling software.

It is simply a customer-facing intake layer that turns incoming requests into clearer, more structured service calls before dispatch.
